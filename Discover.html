Discover Hexham

Ancient abbey. Roman Wall. Independent spirit. A market town that rewards those who take the time to explore it properly.

A town two thousand years in the making

Hexham sits at a remarkable crossroads of history. Founded in 674 AD by St Wilfrid, its abbey has stood watch over the Tyne valley for over thirteen centuries. To the north runs Hadrian's Wall — the northern frontier of the Roman Empire, built nearly two thousand years ago and now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Between the Wall and the town lies some of the finest countryside in northern England: the Tyne valley, the North Pennines, and the wide skies of Northumberland. And at the heart of it all, a thriving market town with independent businesses, a twice-weekly market, and a community that has welcomed visitors for generations.

Getting Here

Hexham is on the Newcastle to Carlisle railway line with regular services. By road, take the A69 from Newcastle (30 mins) or Carlisle (45 mins). The town is well signposted from the A69.

Market Days

Hexham's outdoor market runs every Tuesday and Saturday in the Market Place. One of the oldest markets in the north of England — worth planning your visit around.

Parking

Several car parks in the town centre including Cattle Market, Gilesgate and Alemouth Road. Most offer short and long stay. The town centre is compact and walkable from any car park.

Hadrian's Wall

The Wall runs to the north of Hexham. Key sites include Vindolanda, Chesters Fort and Housesteads. All within 20 minutes by car.

Hexham Abbey

Founded in 674 AD, Hexham Abbey is one of the finest examples of early English architecture in the country. Free to visit. Open daily — check hexhamabbey.org.uk for times.

Tourist Information

For wider visitor information about Hexham and Northumberland, visit visithexham.com or the Northumberland National Park visitor centre at Once Brewed on the Wall.
